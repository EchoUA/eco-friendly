Green Laundering

What ingredients are you using to clean your clothes? If you buy traditional laundry detergent, you may be introducing chemicals to your favorite fabrics. In fact, your laundry detergent may be the most poisonous product in your home!

To make matters worse, the University of Washington found that top-selling laundry products contain at least one chemical regulated as toxic by the federal government, but they are not included on the label at all.

And while other governments require products like laundry detergents to list fragrance ingredients on the label, the US FDA does not. As many as 26 synthetic fragrant chemicals can legally be included in your detergent without your knowledge.3 These fragrance chemicals have been known to irritate skin and even exacerbate conditions such as asthma, but you are NOT required to know about their presence.

Similar to beauty products, the harm of these chemicals do not stop when you wash them down the drain. Materials such as phosphates and synthetic chemicals can wash up in our lakes, rivers, and oceans, polluting our water and damaging the aquatic animals. Clearly, store-bought laundry detergent is not the greenest or healthiest choice for your family. So what can you use to keep your family's clothes clean and green?
